Mahācandra Copper Plate Grant (7th c. CE)

महाचन्द्र-ताम्रपट्ट-शासनम्

TEI-XML
Location: Mysuru, Karnataka Script: Early Kannada Period: 7th century CE Type: Copper Plate Grant Editor: Prof. R. Venkataraman

A land-grant record in Sanskrit (with Kannada endorsements) recording the donation of a village by king Mahācandra to a Śaiva institution. The text is significant for early medieval Karnataka administration and the development of Kannada epigraphy.

Aihole Prashasti of Pulakeśin II (634 CE)

ऐहोळे-प्रशस्तिः

Open
Location: Aihole, Karnataka Script: Chalukyan Brahmi Period: 634 CE Type: Rock Inscription Language: Sanskrit

One of the finest examples of Sanskrit kāvya in epigraphical form, composed by the court poet Rāvikirtti. Records the military victories of Chalukya king Pulakeśin II and contains the earliest known historical reference to Kālidāsa and Bhāravi.

Junagadh Rock Inscription of Rudradāman I (c. 150 CE)

जूनागढ-शिलालेखः रुद्रदामनः

Open
Location: Junagadh, Gujarat Script: Brāhmī Period: c. 150 CE Type: Rock Inscription Language: Classical Sanskrit

The oldest known long inscription in Classical Sanskrit prose (as opposed to Prakrits). Records Rudradāman's restoration of the Sudarśana Lake dam, his military victories, and his patronage of Sanskrit learning. A watershed document in the history of Sanskrit.

Prayagraj Praśasti of Samudragupta (4th c. CE)

प्रयागराज-प्रशस्तिः समुद्रगुप्तस्य

TEI-XML
Location: Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh Script: Gupta Brāhmī Period: 4th century CE Type: Pillar Inscription Editor: Dr. Kavita Sharma

Incised on the Aśokan pillar at Prayagraj, this extensive praśasti in Gupta Brāhmī script is the primary historical source for Samudragupta's conquests and administrative policies. Composed by the court poet Harishena in ornate kāvya Sanskrit.

Thanjavur Big Temple Inscription of Rājarāja I (1010 CE)

Open
Location: Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu Script: Grantha Period: 1010 CE Type: Temple Inscription Language: Sanskrit + Tamil

A bilingual Sanskrit-Tamil record on the walls of the Bṛhadīśvara temple, documenting the elaborate arrangements made by Rājarāja I for temple service, including lists of 400 devadāsī, musicians, and priests endowed for perpetual worship.

Nagarjunakonda Inscription: Buddhist Foundation Record (3rd c. CE)

Restricted
Location: Nagarjunakonda, Andhra Pradesh Script: Brāhmī Period: 3rd century CE Type: Foundation Stone Language: Sanskrit + Prakrit
