    Rasa Theory Beyond the Stage: Ānandavardhana's Vision of the Reader

    Most accounts of rasa theory focus on theatrical performance. But Ānandavardhana's Dhvanyāloka proposes something more radical — a theory of poetic experience centred not on the actor or audience but on the sensitive reader (sahṛdaya). This essay traces that shift and asks what it means for how we read Sanskrit poetry today.
