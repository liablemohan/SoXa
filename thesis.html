Kāvya Aesthetics in Late Classical Sanskrit Poetry: A Re-evaluation of Rasa Theory

रस-सिद्धान्तस्य पुनर्मूल्यांकनम्
Open
Author: Dr. Meera Krishnamurthy Institution: Banaras Hindu University Year: 2024 Degree: Ph.D.

This doctoral thesis examines the evolution of rasa theory across the Nāṭyaśāstra, Dhvanyāloka, and later commentarial traditions, proposing a unified framework for aesthetic experience in classical Sanskrit poetry. Special attention is given to Abhinavagupta's innovations.

The Mahābhāṣya on Pāṇini's Grammar: A Computational Approach to Rule Interaction

पाणिनीयव्याकरणे सङ्गणकीय-अध्ययनम्
Download
Author: Arjun Nambiar Institution: IIT Kanpur Year: 2023 Degree: Ph.D.

A computational formal analysis of rule conflict resolution in Pāṇini's Aṣṭādhyāyī as interpreted in Patañjali's Mahābhāṣya. Using a graph-based algorithm, the thesis resolves over 300 previously disputed rule interactions.

Dharmaśāstra and Legal Pluralism in Colonial India: The Limits of Textual Authority

Open
Author: Priya Balakrishnan Institution: University of Oxford Year: 2022 Degree: D.Phil.

Examines how British colonial administrators selectively translated and applied Dharmaśāstra texts in the Anglo-Indian legal system, arguing that the codification of Hindu law fundamentally distorted the authority and scope of the original Sanskrit sources.
