Our Three Pillars

The Repositories

No existing platform combines all three. The Thesis Repository, Epigraphical Archive, and Literary History database are our primary differentiators — unified under one open-access design system.

📜

Thesis Repository

Doctoral and postgraduate theses in Sanskrit studies from universities across India and the world. Open access with full citations and EPUB downloads.

240+ theses · Open Access
🪨

Epigraphical Archive

Ancient inscriptions from across the Indian subcontinent with diplomatic transcriptions, translations, and location data. Freely browsable and downloadable.

1,200+ inscriptions · TEI-XML
📚

Literary History

A comprehensive, community-authored history of Sanskrit literature from the Vedic period to the present, with scholar contributions and cross-linked references.

80+ articles · Community-authored

Our Purpose

Why SoXa Exists

Every benchmarked Sanskrit platform fails on at least two of four critical criteria: Devanāgarī rendering quality, long-form reading comfort, community contributor visibility, and interactive content discovery. SoXa fills that gap.

🔬

Research Excellence

Supporting scholars with rigorous open-access infrastructure — full citation export, TEI-XML downloads, IAST toggle, and faceted search across all three repositories.

🛡️

Cultural Preservation

Documenting India's epigraphic and literary heritage in stable, open formats that will outlast any individual institution — for future generations of scholars.

🤝

Community First

Contributors write for themselves. The platform is the medium. Every decision — from UI to licensing — is made to serve the contributor, not the institution.
