Current Issue

Linguistics & Grammar

Kārakādhikāra in Pāṇini's Aṣṭādhyāyī: A Formal Re-analysis of Cases 1–4

Pradeep Suryanath & Amitabha Mukhopadhyay
pp. 1–38 · DOI: 10.29381/soxar.2025.001 ·

Applies modern syntactic theory (Dependency Grammar and Government–Binding) to Pāṇini's account of the kāraka relations, arguing that the Aṣṭādhyāyī implicitly encodes a theory of grammatical functions irreducible to traditional case semantics.

Aesthetics & Philosophy

Dhvani and Indirect Meaning: Ānandavardhana's Contribution to a General Theory of Implicature

Dr. Kavita Ramachandran
pp. 39–74 · DOI: 10.29381/soxar.2025.002 ·

A cross-cultural comparison of Ānandavardhana's dhvani theory of poetic resonance with Grice's theory of conversational implicature, arguing for significant structural parallels that illuminate both traditions.

Epigraphy

A New Reading of the Nalanda Copper-plate Grant of Devapāladeva

Dr. Tanvir Ahmed Shaikh
pp. 75–108 · DOI: 10.29381/soxar.2025.003 ·

Offers a fresh epigraphic edition and translation of the Nalanda copper-plate grant, incorporating seventeen previously misread aksharas from infrared photography of the original plates held at the Indian Museum, Kolkata.

Digital Humanities

SanSkRT: A Large-Language-Model-Assisted Corpus for Sanskrit Grammatical Analysis

Nishant Varma, Dr. Anuradha Patel & Sachin Hegde
pp. 109–142 · DOI: 10.29381/soxar.2025.004 ·

Introduces SanSkRT, a 1.2-million-word annotated Sanskrit corpus generated through a hybrid pipeline combining rule-based Pāṇinian tagging and fine-tuned LLM corrections, outperforming existing tools on word-split accuracy by 18%.

History & Culture

Silk Road Sanskrit: Manuscript Evidence for Buddhist Transmission through Central Asia

Prof. Yaroslav Baklanov
pp. 143–188 · DOI: 10.29381/soxar.2025.005 ·

Analyses a newly discovered cache of Sanskrit-Tocharian bilingual manuscript fragments from Dunhuang, revising the scholarly consensus on the date of Mahāyāna text transmission into Central Asia by approximately two centuries.
