Aihole Praśasti of Pulakeśin II

ऐहोळे प्रशस्तिः
Open
Date: 634 CE Script: Old Kannada (Brāhmī derivative) Region: South India (Bagalkot, Karnataka) Provenance: ASI; Epigraphia Indica Vol. VI

The Aihole Praśasti is a Sanskrit eulogistic inscription composed by the court poet Ravikirti. It commemorates the military victories of Cālukya king Pulakeśin II and provides one of the earliest literary references to Kālidāsa and Bhāravi.

Junagadh Rock Inscription of Rudradāman I

जूनागढ-शिलालेखः
Open
Date: c. 150 CE Script: Brāhmī Region: West India (Junagadh, Gujarat) Provenance: ASI; Epigraphia Indica Vol. VIII

The earliest surviving lengthy Sanskrit prose inscription. It commemorates the restoration of the Sudarśana Lake and contains genealogical, administrative, and literary information about the Western Kṣatrapa dynasty. A landmark for the development of Sanskrit as an official language.

Prayagraj Praśasti of Samudragupta

प्रयागस्तम्भ-प्रशस्तिः
Open
Date: c. 350 CE Script: Brāhmī (Gupta) Region: North India (Prayagraj, UP) Author: Harisena (court poet)

Composed by Harisena, the court poet of Samudragupta, and engraved on the Aśokan pillar at Prayagraj. Describes Samudragupta's military campaigns and conquests across all of India, and provides invaluable political and literary information about the early Gupta Empire.
